What We Do

WE DON'T BUY IT.
WE BUILD IT.

A student-run team that designs, manufactures, and races a full off-road vehicle from scratch every season — every weld, every machined part, every decision made by OU students.

Who We Are

SOONER
OFFROAD

Sooner Offroad is the University of Oklahoma's Baja SAE team — a student-run engineering organization that competes annually in the SAE Collegiate Design Series against 100+ universities nationwide.

Every season we design and build a single-seat off-road vehicle entirely from scratch. We don't buy assemblies off the shelf — our team designs the geometry, machines the components, welds the frame, and tunes the car themselves.

The team draws from mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, computer science, and more. If you want real-world experience that goes beyond the classroom, this is where you get it.

At competition, Baja SAE tests our vehicle across a 4-hour endurance race, hill climb, suspension and traction events, maneuverability, and a formal engineering design review judged by industry professionals.

The Competition

WHAT IS
BAJA SAE?

Baja SAE is an intercollegiate engineering design competition organized by SAE International. Each team designs, builds, and races a single-seat off-road vehicle within a strict ruleset — the same ruleset for every school, no matter the budget.

Competition events test every aspect of the vehicle and the team behind it. A typical event includes:

Endurance Race
4-hour wheel-to-wheel race across rough terrain. Reliability and driver endurance are everything.
Hill Climb
Maximum traction and torque output & delivery up a steep, loose-surface grade.
Suspension & Traction
Vehicle geometry and tuning tested over articulation and rough-terrain obstacles.
Maneuverability
Tight cone course testing steering response, low-speed handling, and driver precision.
Design Review
Formal presentation to industry engineers judging design decisions, analysis, and innovation.
Cost Report
Detailed accounting of every component — manufacturing costs, material choices, and design trade-offs.
